# PhysiqueOS Personas

## Purpose

These are not marketing personas.

They are Intelligence Personas.

They exist to continuously test the Evidence Engine, onboarding philosophy, Daily Briefing, recommendations, and operating-system behavior.

Every major update to PhysiqueOS should be evaluated against these personas.

The purpose is not to predict every possible user.

The purpose is to keep the product honest across a wide range of real human goals, evidence histories, constraints, preferences, and engagement styles.

If a feature improves one persona while degrading another, that tradeoff should be intentional.

These personas should become the long-term intelligence test suite for PhysiqueOS.

---

# Persona Format

Each persona includes:

* short background
* primary goal
* potential supporting objectives
* existing evidence
* missing evidence
* likely protocols
* preferred engagement style
* potential integrations
* likely Daily Briefing style
* potential challenges
* onboarding priorities
* inference opportunities
* questions to avoid
* first wow moment
* first Daily Briefing emphasis
* six-week Daily Briefing evolution

The goal is not to create rigid product paths.

The goal is to exercise the intelligence model.

# 1. Visible Abs Founder

Short Background:

A highly motivated user pursuing visible abs with DEXA history, weight history, progress photos, peptides, nutrition context, and strong daily adherence.

Primary Goal:

Visible abs at rest.

Potential Supporting Objectives:

Preserve lean mass, maintain 8-9% body fat, keep progress photos consistent, maintain protocol adherence, protect recovery.

Existing Evidence:

DEXA scans, historical weights, progress photos, protocol schedules, manual nutrition range.

Missing Evidence:

Full nutrition logs, wearable recovery history, training volume, lab work.

Likely Protocols:

Morning weigh-ins, weekly photos, peptide schedules, protein target, foam rolling.

Preferred Engagement Style:

Fast, direct, high-signal, evidence-aware.

Potential Integrations:

Apple Health, BodySpec, Cronometer, Garmin, photo library.

Likely Daily Briefing Style:

Specific, confident, trajectory-oriented, honest about remaining uncertainty.

Potential Challenges:

Avoid overexplaining. Avoid turning Home into a dashboard. Avoid repeating the same briefing.

Onboarding Should Prioritize:

Importing evidence, explaining the active goal model, and showing immediate understanding.

PhysiqueOS Should Infer:

Goal phase, evidence cadence, supporting objectives, confidence drivers.

PhysiqueOS Should Avoid Asking:

Questions already answered by uploaded DEXA, photos, or weight history.

First Wow Moment:

The first Daily Briefing connects DEXA, weight trend, photos, and protocols into one coherent trajectory.

First Daily Briefing Might Emphasize:

The cut is supported by multiple evidence streams, with lean-mass preservation as the key quality objective.

After Six Weeks:

The briefing references personal response patterns, evidence freshness, protocol adherence, and visual progression.

# How Personas Should Be Used

These personas should be used as an intelligence test suite.

Every major product decision should be evaluated against them.

Questions to ask:

* Does this feature help this persona make a better decision?
* Does it ask for evidence the persona already has elsewhere?
* Does it create unnecessary manual work?
* Does it respect the persona's preferred engagement style?
* Does it connect evidence back to the persona's goals?
* Does it improve Daily Briefing quality?
* Does it improve confidence or merely collect data?
* Does it preserve user autonomy?
* Does it work for users with little evidence and users with years of evidence?
* Does it support passive, voice, manual, and integration-based evidence capture?

If a feature improves one persona while degrading another, that tradeoff should be intentionally evaluated.

For example:

* A highly detailed dashboard may help the Data Enthusiast but overwhelm the First Fat-Loss Journey user.
* Aggressive coaching may help the Proactive Guidance user but violate the Aggregation-Only user's preference.
* Wearable-first design may help the Biohacker but exclude the Refuses Wearables persona.
* A minimal Daily Briefing may help the Busy Executive but underserve the Bodybuilder preparing for a show.

The goal is not to design every feature for every persona equally.

The goal is to understand the tradeoffs.

PhysiqueOS should remain flexible enough to learn different users without becoming a different product for each one.

The long-term standard is simple:

> Every persona should feel that PhysiqueOS understands their goal, respects their context, and improves their next decision.
